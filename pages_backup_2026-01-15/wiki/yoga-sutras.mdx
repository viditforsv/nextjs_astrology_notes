# Yoga Sutras

The Yoga Sutras of Maharishi Patanjali is a foundational text of classical Yoga philosophy, consisting of 196 aphorisms that systematically present the theory and practice of Yoga.

## Structure of the Yoga Sutras

### Samadhi Pada (Chapter on Contemplation)
- Definition of Yoga
- Types of Samadhi (meditative absorption)
- Obstacles to Yoga practice

### Sadhana Pada (Chapter on Practice)
- **Yamas and Niyamas** (ethical foundations)
- Asana (posture)
- Pranayama (breath control)
- Pratyahara (withdrawal of senses)

### Vibhuti Pada (Chapter on Accomplishments)
- Dharana (concentration)
- Dhyana (meditation)
- Samadhi (absorption)
- Siddhis (spiritual powers)

### Kaivalya Pada (Chapter on Liberation)
- Nature of liberation
- Freedom from karma
- Ultimate state of consciousness

## The Eight Limbs of Yoga (Ashtanga)

1. **Yamas** - Ethical restraints
2. **Niyamas** - Personal observances
3. **Asana** - Physical postures
4. **Pranayama** - Breath control
5. **Pratyahara** - Sense withdrawal
6. **Dharana** - Concentration
7. **Dhyana** - Meditation
8. **Samadhi** - Absorption

## Connection to Vedic Astrology

### The Sacred Connection

The **5 Yamas** and **5 Niyamas** correspond to the five dimensions of human experience, creating a complete framework for personal development that works in harmony with astrological understanding.
