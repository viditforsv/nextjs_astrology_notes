## Characteristics

Revati represents completion, nourishment, and safe journey. As the final nakshatra, it encompasses all experiences and offers protection and prosperity.

### Positive Traits
- Kind and compassionate
- Nurturing and protective
- Wealthy and prosperous
- Love for travel and journey
- Independent and self-sufficient
- Artistic and creative
- Spiritual and intuitive
- Good social skills

### Negative Traits
- Can be overly sensitive
- May be too idealistic
- Difficulty with practical matters
- Can be gullible or naive
- May lack self-confidence
- Tendency to be overly dependent

## Special Notes

Revati is excellent for:
- Travel and journeys
- Marriages and unions
- Starting businesses
- Charitable activities
- Creative projects
- Spiritual practices
- Completing projects

The fish swimming in opposite directions represents:
- Duality of existence (material/spiritual)
- Journey through the ocean of life
- Ability to navigate both worlds
- Completion leading to new beginning (last nakshatra leading back to first)

**Special Characteristics**:
- Last nakshatra of the zodiac - represents completion and fulfillment
- Very auspicious for most activities
- Gives wealth, especially through travel or foreign connections
- Natural talent for arts and music
- Strong protective energy

People born under Revati:
- Are gentle and kind-hearted
- Have refined tastes
- Love luxury and comfort
- Often wealthy or achieve prosperity
- Excellent hosts and caregivers
- Natural counselors
- May travel extensively

**Mercury + Pisces**:
- Mercury in Pisces is debilitated but Revati elevates it
- Gives imagination, creativity, artistic ability
- Intuitive communication rather than logical
- Poetic and musical expression

**Special Significance**:
- Lord Krishna departed from earthly life during Revati nakshatra
- Represents the end of one cycle and beginning of another
- Associated with safe passage from one realm to another
- Excellent for moksha (liberation) pursuits

This is one of the most benevolent and auspicious nakshatras. It represents the completion of the soul's journey through all 27 nakshatras, bringing together all experiences with wisdom, compassion, and prosperity.

**Marriage**: Revati is considered excellent for marriage - brings harmony, prosperity, and protection to the union.

The journey from Ashwini (beginning) to Revati (completion) represents the soul's evolution through all life experiences, finally reaching a state of wisdom, compassion, and readiness for transcendence.
