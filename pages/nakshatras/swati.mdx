## Characteristics

Swati represents independence, flexibility, and the power of the wind. It's associated with freedom, trade, and the ability to adapt and move.

### Positive Traits
- Independent and self-reliant
- Adaptable and flexible
- Diplomatic and tactful
- Business-minded
- Social and friendly
- Balanced and fair
- Good communicators
- Love for freedom

### Negative Traits
- Can be restless and scattered
- Difficulty with commitment
- May be indecisive
- Overly influenced by others
- Can be superficial in relationships
- May lack roots or stability

## Ruling Deity: Vayu

Vayu (the Wind God) represents:
- Freedom and movement
- Breath and life force (Prana)
- Change and transformation
- Communication and dispersal

Swati is excellent for:

- Travel and relocation
- Networking events

The sword blown by wind symbolizes something that looks firm but moves with the breeze - independence combined with flexibility. People born under Swati value their freedom above all and need space to explore and grow. They are natural traders and excel in fields requiring diplomacy and adaptability.

Swati is Mahatma Gandhi's birth nakshatra, exemplifying its qualities of peaceful resistance, diplomacy, and independence.
